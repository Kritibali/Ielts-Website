Texts come from books,journals,magazines,newspapers and online resources, written for a non-specialist audience. all topics are of general interst to students at undergraduate or postgraduate level. The texts may be written in different styles, for example narrative, descriptive or discursive. If texts use technical vocabulary ,then a simple dictionary definition is provided.

You should be careful when writing your answer sheet because you'll lose marks for incorrect spelling and grammar.

1. Reading paper


Summary :
Time Allowed : 60 Min(including transfer time)
No. of Sections : 3 ; total length is 150 words
No. of Questions : 40
Marking : Each correct answer give 1 mark


Types of questions :

  • Mutiple Choice
  • Identifying information(True/False/Not given)
  • Identifying writer's views/claims
  • Matching Information
  • Matching Headings
  • Matching Features
  • Matching sentence endings
  • sentence completion
  • Summary /note/table/flow chart completion

2. Writing Paper


There are two writing tasks and both must be completed.

Task 1-
You have to describe some visual information in your own words. Atleast 150 words should be written in 20 min.
Task 2-
You are given a point of view, argument or problem which you need to discuss . Atleast 250 words should be written in about 40 minutes.

3. Listening paper


 This paper has four sections,with ten questions in each section.
Section 1 and 2 deal with everyday , social situations.There is a conversation between two speakers in this section.
Section 3 and 4 deal with educational and training situations.

Types of Questions

  • Multiple choice
  • Matching
  • Plan/Map/diagram labelling
  • Form/note/table/flow/chart/summary completion
  • Sentence completion
  • Short answer Question

4. Speaking Paper


The Speaking test is a face-to-face interview between the candidate and an examiner.There are three parts to the test which are given below:

Part 1 - Introduction and interview
Part 2 - Long turn
Part 3 - Discussion
